Building Blocks of Algebra

\

The "mystery box" has a special name in math. Understanding these key terms is the first step to becoming an expert equation solver. These are the fundamental rules of algebra.

\
\
\

Variable

\

In math, we use a letter (like $x$) to represent an unknown number. This is our "mystery box." So, instead of $4 + ? = 10$, we write: $4 + x = 10$

\
\
\

Equation

\

An equation is a statement that two things are equal. The equals sign (=) is the center of our balanced scale. It means the value on the left side is exactly the same as the value on the right side.

\
\
\
\

The Golden Rule of Equations

\

To keep the scale balanced, whatever you do to one side of an equation, you **MUST** do to the other side.

\
